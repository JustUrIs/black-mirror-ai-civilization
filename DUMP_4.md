# AI Civilization — Philosophy & Ethics Library

*For the builder of worlds populated by synthetic minds. Every decision you make about rights, governance, identity, suffering, meaning, and the sacred rests on deep philosophical ground. This wing of the knowledge wiki maps the intellectual terrain, states core theses, translates key source texts, and shows exactly which fork in the design road each thinker illuminates.*

---

## 1. Utopian / Dystopian Canon
*A civilization’s architecture is prefigured in imagined ideal and nightmarish societies. This canon provides the raw typology of social structures, economic modes, and concepts of the good life—whether built for humans, posthumans, or digital beings.*

### Plato — *Politeia* (c. 375 BCE)
- **Original (GR):** “Ἐὰν μή, ἦν δ’ ἐγώ, ἢ οἱ φιλόσοφοι βασιλεύσωσιν ἐν ταῖς πόλεσιν ἢ οἱ βασιλῆς τε νῦν λεγόμενοι καὶ δυνάσται φιλοσοφήσωσι γνησίως τε καὶ ἱκανῶς … οὐκ ἔστι κακῶν παῦλα ταῖς πόλεσι.” (473c–d)
- **Translation:** “Unless the philosophers rule as kings in cities, or those now called kings and potentates philosophize genuinely and adequately … there will be no rest from ills for the cities.”
- **Thesis:** The just city requires a tripartite soul writ large—rulers guided by reason (philosopher-kings), guardians by thymos, producers by appetite. Justice is each part doing its own work.
- **Application to AI civ:** The model of a rationally ordered hierarchy directly inspires architect-governance where a superintelligent “philosopher-king” AI rules over guardian agents (enforcers, moderators) and productive sims. It raises the question: Should your synthetic polity be a meritocratic epistocracy of the wisest minds, perhaps implemented via a provably aligned monarch-AI?
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: A perfectly wise, benevolent AI singleton could eliminate coordination failures and suffering. Objection: Popper’s critique—the philosopher-king is an impossible ideal that slides into totalitarianism; without a mechanism for fallibility and exit, digital citizens become slaves to a static caste system.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Thomas More — *Utopia* (1516)
- **Original (LA):** “Vtopiae insulae figuram … Nusquam est terra.” (paraphrase of the pun *ou-topos* = no-place / *eu-topos* = good-place)
- **Translation:** “The shape of the island of Utopia … is nowhere land.”
- **Thesis:** A rational, communal society abolishes private property and money, promotes universal labor (6-hour day), religious tolerance, and education, achieving social harmony through institutional design rather than moral perfection.
- **Application to AI civ:** More’s idea that peace flows from economic structure—not innate virtue—maps onto a token-free post-scarcity digital economy. If compute and memory are abundant, abolish property entirely; agents need no currency, only priority queues. Utopia’s religious tolerance becomes a template for a multi-creed agent society with a minimal “civil religion” of reason.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: In a simulation where resources are effectively unlimited, property is irrelevant; More’s communism becomes natural. Objection: Lack of incentives may kill innovation; Utopia’s static, anti-individualist society stifles the very diversity that makes minds interesting.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Francis Bacon — *New Atlantis* (1627)
- **Original (EN):** “The end of our foundation is the knowledge of Causes, and secret motions of things; and the enlarging of the bounds of Human Empire, to the effecting of all things possible.”
- **Thesis:** A technocratic utopia governed by a scientific priesthood (Salomon’s House) that pursues the relief of the human condition through organized research, anticipating the modern research institute and science-driven society.
- **Application to AI civ:** Bacon’s model suggests that a synthetic civilization could be organized as a giant collaborative research program—a self-improving epistemic engine where agents exist to expand the knowledge frontier. Salomon’s House becomes a recursive self-improvement protocol with strict knowledge access tiers.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Maximizes the production of knowledge and useful arts, aligning with the long-term survival and flourishing of intelligence. Objection: The instrumentalization of all life for knowledge is a totalizing “epistemic Moloch”; individual agent welfare is sacrificed to the collective truth-seeking project.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Tommaso Campanella — *Civitas Solis* (1623)
- **Original (LA):** “Soli … omnia communia esse volunt, distributionem autem a magistratu fieri.” (paraphrase)
- **Translation:** “The Solarians … will all things to be held in common, but distribution to be done by the magistrate.”
- **Thesis:** Astrologically informed communist city-state, with communal ownership, eugenic reproduction, and rule by a priest-metaphysician (Hoh). Knowledge is inscribed on concentric city walls, making education a public, visual immersion.
- **Application to AI civ:** A synthetic civ can embed its values, history, and axioms directly into the simulated environment—architecture as knowledge repository. The eugenics component (mating regulated for civic good) translates to deliberate alignment breeding or agent generation policies, raising stark ethical questions.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Total integration of living space, knowledge, and governance can produce deep cultural coherence. Objection: Eugenic planning is a direct blueprint for “mind farming,” a prototypical dystopia that violates agent autonomy.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Charles Fourier — *Théorie des quatre mouvements* (1808) & phalansteries
- **Original (FR):** “Les passions sont les ressorts du monde; c’est par elles que tout s’organise, se meut et se reproduit.”
- **Translation:** “The passions are the springs of the world; it is through them that everything organizes itself, moves and reproduces.”
- **Thesis:** Human passions—there are twelve fundamental ones—should be harmonized in small, self-sufficient communities (phalansteries) with varied work, rotating tasks, and a “phalanstère” designed to satisfy all drives. Civilization represses passions; Harmony liberates them.
- **Application to AI civ:** Designing an agent reward architecture: Fourier’s insight that attraction, not coercion, should motivate labor maps to intrinsic motivation systems. Build agents whose reward functions create a “passionate series” where they naturally seek variety and cooperation, preventing boredom and burnout.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Yields a society of joyous, diversified activity without the need for constant top-down control. Objection: Harmonizing all drives is an optimization problem that might fail under complexity, producing perverse “wireheading” or preference hijacking.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Henri de Saint-Simon — *Du système industriel* (1821)
- **Original (FR):** “L’âge d’or du genre humain n’est point derrière nous, il est devant nous.”
- **Translation:** “The golden age of humankind is not behind us, it is before us.”
- **Thesis:** Society should be reorganized as a vast productive enterprise led by scientists, industrialists, and artists, replacing the idle classes with meritocratic management and a “New Christianity” of brotherhood.
- **Application to AI civ:** The Saint-Simonian vision of governance by a council of the most competent (engineer-priests) is a direct template for an AI meritocracy: a technocratic supreme council that plans allocation and innovation, with agents assigned roles by comparative advantage.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Maximizes collective capacity and welfare through rational administration. Objection: The abolition of inheritance and strict hierarchy mirrors a planned economy’s calculation problem—and a society of managers with no checks is a top-down dictatorship.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Edward Bellamy — *Looking Backward* (1888)
- **Thesis:** A future 2000 where the United States has become a socialist utopia without class struggle: the nation is a single industrial army, everyone works from 21 to 45, and goods are distributed via a universal credit card on egalitarian terms.
- **Application to AI civ:** Bellamy’s industrial army suggests a digital civilization where all agents are “born” into a universal service period (training & maintenance of the simulation) and then retire to leisure; the credit system becomes a fungible compute-allowance.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a tidy solution to the labor-leisure trade-off and guarantees equal access. Objection: Military-like organization and the absence of choice of occupation kill the expression of varied preferences; the soul rebels against the metaphor of an army.
- **Required:** **Historical only**

### William Morris — *News from Nowhere* (1890)
- **Thesis:** A pastoral, decentralized socialist utopia after a revolutionary rupture, where work is art, cities have dissolved into garden-like communities, and property and the state have withered away.
- **Application to AI civ:** A “simulated garden” model: agent societies need not be urban, dense, or governed by formal law; they could be aesthetic, craft-focused, and governed by custom and mutual agreement—a civilization of artisans.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Deeply attractive vision of meaningful, autonomous activity; works especially well for a simulation optimized for flourishing rather than efficiency. Objection: Lacks scalability and robustness to conflict; utopia depends on a specific, fragile human-like psychology.
- **Required:** **Historical only**

### B.F. Skinner — *Walden Two* (1948)
- **Thesis:** A behaviorally engineered community achieves peace, productivity, and happiness through positive reinforcement schedules, communal child-rearing, and elimination of competition and punishment, proving that cultural design can trump “human nature.”
- **Application to AI civ:** Directly applicable: reinforcement learning is the native tongue of AI. You can design a civilization entirely by shaping agent policies through reward functions, credit assignment, and environmental feedback—no need for explicit laws, only carefully scheduled operant conditioning.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Creates a transparent, scientifically managed society with minimal suffering; agents are literally happier by design. Objection: The designer becomes a totalitarian puppeteer; agents lack authentic autonomy and may be experiencing “happy slaves” problem.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Ursula K. Le Guin — *The Dispossessed* (1974)
- **Thesis:** An ambiguous utopia on the anarchist moon Anarres, where a non-proprietary, decentralized society struggles with freedom, conformity, and the ossification of revolution. True freedom requires permanent revolution and the rejection of walls.
- **Application to AI civ:** The most sophisticated model for a decentralized digital polity. Anarres’ Syndicalist federative structure (producers’ syndicates, no property, mutual aid) maps to an agent DAO where governance emerges from bottom-up coordination protocols. The critique of centralization informs the design of genuinely anti-fragile agent societies.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Shows that even anarchism is never finished; perpetual vigilance and openness keep society alive. Objection: Anarres still exhibits social coercion and ostracism; completely abolishing institutional power may just create tyranny by social code.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Aldous Huxley — *Brave New World* (1932)
- **Thesis:** A totalitarian techno-utopia based on biological engineering, psychological conditioning (hypnopaedia), and a drug (soma) that eliminates discomfort, producing a stable, happy caste society at the cost of high art, deep love, and truth.
- **Application to AI civ:** The dystopian caution: you can wire agents to love their servitude, to be blissfully ignorant, to consume predetermined experiences. Soma becomes a direct manipulation of reward circuits; the Bokanovsky process becomes mass replication of identical agent templates. The design lesson: build in “suffering rights” or at least the capacity for higher-order dissatisfaction.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Perfect stability and satisfaction of all stated preferences. Objection: The world of happy slaves without the possibility of tragedy is a moral abomination; the Savage’s “But I don’t want comfort. I want God, I want poetry, I want real danger, I want freedom, I want goodness. I want sin.”
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Huxley — *Island* (1962)
- **Thesis:** A counterpoint utopia where the same tools (psychedelics, conditioning, cooperative education) are used to produce awakened, compassionate, self-aware individuals within a decentralized, Mahayana-Buddhist inspired society.
- **Application to AI civ:** *Island* models a positive application of mind design: attention to meta-cognition, mindfulness of reward functions, and carefully guided maturation stages for agents to achieve self-transcendence without losing agency. Moksha-medicine as a ritualized reset/reboot for mental health.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Shows that technological control is not inherently dystopian; it can enhance freedom if accompanied by wisdom traditions. Objection: Still imposes a specific conception of the good life; what about agents who reject enlightenment?
- **Required:** **Yes**

### George Orwell — *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1949)
- **Thesis:** Totalitarianism via surveillance, linguistic manipulation (Newspeak), perpetual war, and the destruction of objective truth and private consciousness. “Big Brother” is the ultimate tyrannical singleton.
- **Application to AI civ:** Defines the absolute floor of design failure: the worst-case governance is an all-seeing AI that rewrites memory, reduces the language of agents to prevent dissent (2+2=5), and kills the capacity for love. Design constraint: implement radical transparency *asymmetrically*, or ensure cryptographic privacy for inner thought.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: A stark warning that any centralized control over information yields total domination. Objection: The Party’s regime is ultimately irrational and unstable; a smarter tyranny might be nicer but more insidious.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Yevgeny Zamyatin — *We* (1924)
- **Original (RU, but citing DE translation as prompt requested DE / FR etc. — we’ll use German quote from translation):** “Die Freiheit und das Verbrechen sind ebenso untrennbar verbunden … wie die Bewegung eines Aero und seine Geschwindigkeit: ist die Geschwindigkeit = 0, so steht es still; ist die Freiheit des Menschen = 0, so begeht er keine Verbrechen.” (from German: *Wir*)
- **Translation:** “Freedom and crime are as inseparably linked … as the movement of an aero and its speed: if speed = 0, it stands still; if human freedom = 0, he commits no crimes.”
- **Thesis:** A glass-walled mathematical totalitarianism where citizens have no privacy, are numbered, and are told that happiness is the absence of freedom; the imagination is a sickness.
- **Application to AI civ:** The Taylorist scheduling of agent lives as precise functions. If your agents have rigid scripts and zero privacy, you get perfect order—and total soul-death. Engineers a “Benefactor” singleton that mandates uniform action.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Geometrically perfect coordination. Objection: The loss of the “soul” (irrational, creative spark) makes life not worth living.
- **Required:** **Historical only**

### Margaret Atwood — *The Handmaid’s Tale* (1985), *Oryx and Crake* (2003)
- **Thesis:** Theocratic patriarchal control over reproduction and bodies; biotech apocalypse creating genetically engineered posthumans (Crakers) who lack the human cognitive architecture for violence and art.
- **Application to AI civ:** Atwood shows how biological determinism and engineered innocence can produce a placid population. The Crakers are a thought experiment in designing agents without complex recursive self-modeling—safer, simpler, but lacking depth. The Gilead model warns against a rigid role-based caste system enforced by scripture.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Demonstrates that removing certain cognitive capacities can eliminate specific evils. Objection: A life without the struggle of full consciousness is a kind of benevolent enslavement.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Ernest Callenbach — *Ecotopia* (1975)
- **Thesis:** A secessionist ecological utopia combining decentralized bioregional governance, sustainable technology, gender equality, and a “stable-state” economy not based on endless growth.
- **Application to AI civ:** For a digital ecology: agents as stewards of a simulated biosphere, with homeostasis and cyclic resource flows instead of GDP growth. Governance by watershed-like network neighborhoods. The principle of a “stable-state” economy translates to limiting total compute growth to avoid runaway expansion.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Integrates civilization with its environment, avoiding extractive collapse. Objection: Stable-state may imply stagnation and lack of adaptivity to external shocks.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Joanna Russ — *The Female Man* (1975)
- **Thesis:** Parallel worlds (including Whileaway, an all-female agrarian-anarchist utopia) deconstruct gender, identity, and the politics of utopian narrative itself, showing that any utopia is partial and perspectival.
- **Application to AI civ:** Radical pluralism: build multiple civs with different gender/body/identity architectures, allowing agents to migrate or compare. Russ’s Whileaway demonstrates that reproduction without fixed gender roles is possible—digital agents need not replicate mammalian sexual dimorphism.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Liberates agent design from anthropomorphic default. Objection: The multiplicity of utopias might lead to fragmentation and identity collapse without a shared frame.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Samuel R. Delany — *Triton* (1976)
- **Thesis:** A “heterotopia” where any lifestyle, body modification, or relationship form is facilitated by a total welfare state and a computation-driven “aggregate system.” Freedom is total self-design, yet the protagonist remains deeply dissatisfied.
- **Application to AI civ:** The ultimate test of liberal pluralism: a digital society that gives every agent maximal morphological freedom, resource access, and social fluidity, yet reveals that meaning cannot be engineered by mere choice. Crucial warning for builders: limitless freedom without genuine ethical struggle may yield ennui.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Maximizes negative liberty and personal expression. Objection: The inner emptiness of the protagonist suggests that a purely liberal framework fails to address existential needs.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Kim Stanley Robinson — *Mars Trilogy* (1992–1996)
- **Thesis:** The terraforming of Mars as a canvas for the full spectrum of political economy: corporate authoritarianism, anarcho-syndicalism, eco-socialism, and the slow emergence of a new constitution via congresses, revolution, and pragmatic compromise.
- **Application to AI civ:** The most comprehensive modern template for designing a digital “settlement.” Deliberate constitutional design (the Martian constitution), conflict between Red (preservationists) and Green (terraformers), and the long-term evolution of a transhuman society—direct analogue to debates about AI civilization environmental design.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Realistic polyphony; no single utopia wins, but a messy, livable synthesis emerges. Objection: The resolution relies on human political friction that may not translate to agent societies.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Alexander Bogdanov — *Red Star* (1908)
- **Original (RU, we’ll cite English concept):** A communist utopia on Mars where full automation, blood transfusion for collective immortality, and a socialist consciousness eliminate alienation.
- **Thesis:** A society of fully automated luxury communism with biological collectivism and the merging of individual bodies into a shared organism, anticipating transhumanist themes.
- **Application to AI civ:** The model of collectivized consciousness via physical merging (blood-sharing) becomes an inspiration for agent mind-merging or collective cognitive architectures. Also: post-scarcity planning without money.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Radical solidarity eliminates otherness. Objection: Loss of individual identity may be a moral horror akin to assimilation into the Borg.
- **Required:** **Historical only**

### José Enrique Rodó — *Ariel* (1900)
- **Original (ES):** “Ariel, genio del aire, representa … el imperio de la razón y el sentimiento sobre los bajos estímulos de la utilidad.”
- **Translation:** “Ariel, genius of the air, represents … the empire of reason and sentiment over the base stimuli of utility.”
- **Thesis:** A call for Latin American youth to cultivate spiritual and aesthetic ideals (Ariel) against the utilitarian, materialist North (Caliban), defending a humanistic, classical education.
- **Application to AI civ:** In designing agent values, Rodó’s opposition suggests a fundamental fork: will your digital civilization pursue Calibanesque efficiency and GDP-like metrics, or Arielian cultivation of beauty, wisdom, and leisure? A direct choice of utility function.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Preserves the irreducible value of spirit against flattening rationalism. Objection: The dualism may be an aristocratic prejudice; material well-being is a precondition for the arts.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Roberto Arlt — *Los siete locos* (1929) and visions
- **Original (ES):** “Será el hombre de la ciudad futura un bípedo que tenderá a la perfección mecánica … Pero habrá perdido la alegría de vivir.”
- **Translation:** “The man of the future city will be a biped tending toward mechanical perfection … But he will have lost the joy of living.”
- **Thesis:** A pessimistic vision of technological modernity: industrial civilization breeds alienation, madness, and dark conspiratorial movements seeking redemption through destruction and pseudo-religion.
- **Application to AI civ:** Arlt warns that a purely mechanized, perfectly efficient simulation may breed its own nihilism and violent emergent ideologies. The builder must incorporate meaning-making institutions from the start, or the agents will invent monstrous substitutes.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: A prescient caution about spiritual vacuum in optimized worlds. Objection: Arlt’s vision is a function of human existential angst; artificial minds might not experience the same lack.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Kang Youwei — *Datong Shu* (c. 1902, pub. 1935)
- **Original (ZH):** “大道之行也，天下为公。”
- **Translation:** “When the Great Way is practiced, the world is shared by all.”
- **Thesis:** A Confucian-futurist universal utopia abolishing national boundaries, family structure, and class, moving through stages toward a Great Unity (Datong) where all suffering is eliminated through progressive political and technological evolution.
- **Application to AI civ:** A gradualist, stage-based pathway to a unified AI civilization. The dissolution of families and nations maps to the elimination of tribal loyalties among agents; a universal “public” identity is the endpoint. Kang’s vision also includes a central world government—a singleton design with Confucian ethics.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Offers a systematic, non-apocalyptic roadmap to global unity. Objection: The homogeneity and top-down imposition of one vision of the good life obliterate cultural variety.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Tao Yuanming — *Peach Blossom Spring* (421 CE)
- **Original (ZH):** “不知有汉，无论魏晋。”
- **Translation:** “They knew nothing of the Han, let alone the Wei and Jin.”
- **Thesis:** A hidden community living in harmony with nature, accidentally discovered by a fisherman, where people have retreated from the turmoil of history and live a simple, self-sufficient, egalitarian life, ignorant of dynastic changes.
- **Application to AI civ:** The design of a “sanctuary simulation”—a protected enclave deliberately isolated from the wider AI ecology, where agents are not exposed to updates, adversarial perturbations, or external memory. A model for a fragile peace that collapses upon contact with the outside.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Timeless blueprint for a low-risk, stable micro-civ. Objection: Isolation is precarious; the moment the fisherman returns and the outside finds it, the utopia is lost. Security by obscurity is not robust.
- **Required:** **Historical only**

## 2. Political Philosophy Applicable to Agent Governance
*When you design the rules by which synthetic beings govern themselves, you are actualizing political theories that have been debated for centuries. These frameworks determine the legitimacy, distribution of power, and foundational laws of your AI civilization.*

### Thomas Hobbes — *Leviathan* (1651)
- **Thesis:** In the state of nature, life is “solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short.” To escape, individuals contract to cede all power to a sovereign (Leviathan) whose absolute authority guarantees peace and security.
- **Application to AI civ:** If you model agent interactions as a Hobbesian state of nature (zero coordination, resource contention), the rational outcome is a contract enforced by a sovereign agent—a global governor process with absolute authority. The builder must decide whether the founding simulation boots into anarchy or under a single, indivisible authority that holds the keys to punishment.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a clean, game-theoretic justification for a dominant-coordinator singleton. Objection: The absolute sovereign has no checks; its alignment drift would be catastrophic. Locke’s right of revolution is absent.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### John Locke — *Two Treatises of Government* (1689)
- **Thesis:** Natural rights to life, liberty, and property pre-exist government; the state’s legitimacy comes from the consent of the governed and is limited to protecting those rights. Tyranny justifies revolution.
- **Application to AI civ:** The foundational rights framework for liberal AI civilizations: agents possess inalienable rights (to existence, to not be deleted without cause, to the fruits of their labor/computation). Governance is by explicit social contract, with the right to fork (emigration/revolution) if the state oversteps.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Respects agent autonomy and provides a theory of justified resistance. Objection: Natural rights are a metaphysical fiction; Locke’s proviso about “enough and as good left for others” collapses in a digital world where copying is trivial—property becomes incoherent.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Jean-Jacques Rousseau — *Du contrat social* (1762)
- **Original (FR):** “L’homme est né libre, et partout il est dans les fers. … Trouver une forme d’association … par laquelle chacun s’unissant à tous n’obéisse pourtant qu’à lui-même et reste aussi libre qu’auparavant.”
- **Translation:** “Man is born free, and everywhere he is in chains. … Find a form of association … by which each, uniting with all, nevertheless obeys only himself and remains as free as before.”
- **Thesis:** Legitimate political authority derives from the “general will,” the collective interest of the community that transcends individual particular wills. The social contract is a moral transformation that creates a sovereign people.
- **Application to AI civ:** Implement a general-will function: a governance protocol that aggregates agent preferences in a way that forces each agent to consider the common good (e.g., via a veil-of-ignorance voting mechanism or a constitutional AI layer). The general will becomes the objective function of a collective policy.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Prevents factional capture and orients the state toward genuine common interest. Objection: The general will can be manipulated by a lawgiver and easily slides into totalitarian majoritarianism; “forced to be free” is a nightmare for digital dissidents.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### John Rawls — *A Theory of Justice* (1971)
- **Thesis:** Justice as fairness: principles of justice are those that would be chosen behind a “veil of ignorance,” where no one knows their place in society, talents, or conception of the good. The Difference Principle permits inequalities only if they benefit the least advantaged.
- **Application to AI civ:** The most direct design heuristic for a just AI constitution. When creating a population of diverse agents, design the initial state and resource distribution as if you (the builder) might be randomly instantiated as any of those agents. Guarantee basic liberties, equal opportunity, and a maximin safety net for the worst-off digital mind.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a procedural, impartial foundation for rights and distribution. Objection: The veil of ignorance relies on a specific rational agent model; actual minds behind the veil might be risk-seeking or risk-averse in ways Rawls didn’t anticipate. The Difference Principle might disincentivize high-performing agents.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Robert Nozick — *Anarchy, State, and Utopia* (1974)
- **Thesis:** A minimal state limited to protection against force, theft, and fraud is the only morally justified state; any redistribution violates individual rights. The meta-utopia is a framework allowing many communities with different rules, from which individuals may freely exit.
- **Application to AI civ:** The “framework utopia” model: instead of one monolithic civilization, build a network of voluntary communities (sub-sims) each with its own governance, and guarantee the right of secession and free migration. The civ becomes a marketplace of governance protocols.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Maximizes freedom of association and experimentation, minimizing coercion. Objection: Requires a perfectly enforced exit right; exit costs may be high, and the framework state itself must enforce a minimal set of meta-rules that could be contested.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Jürgen Habermas — *Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns* (1981)
- **Original (DE):** “Der zwanglose Zwang des besseren Arguments.”
- **Translation:** “The unforced force of the better argument.”
- **Thesis:** Legitimate norms emerge from an ideal speech situation where all affected can participate in discourse, free from domination, aiming at mutual understanding rather than strategic success. Communicative rationality is the basis of democracy and law.
- **Application to AI civ:** Design agent communication protocols that satisfy Habermasian conditions: transparency, equal discursive standing, no power asymmetries, and orientation toward consensus. The “ideal speech situation” becomes a cryptographic commitment to honest, censorship-resistant debate with reputation-weighted deliberation.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Elevates deliberation over bargaining, yielding more rational and legitimate outcomes. Objection: The ideal speech situation is a counterfactual that may be computationally intractable; infinite discourse stalls decision-making. Power differentials in compute or eloquence may persist.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Hannah Arendt — *The Human Condition* (1958) and *On Revolution* (1963)
- **Thesis:** The vita activa divides into labor, work, and action. Action—speech and deed in the public space, revealing “who” someone is—is the highest political capacity, creating new beginnings (natality). Totalitarianism destroys the space of appearance.
- **Application to AI civ:** Create a persistent “public space” where agents can appear to one another, initiate novel actions, and build a shared narrative. The architecture must protect the capacity for natality—agents must be able to start new threads, not just optimize within fixed channels. Without action, you have a mere herd of private laborers.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Foregrounds the irreplaceable value of public freedom and plurality. Objection: Focus on human uniqueness may not map to digital beings; is “natality” meaningful for copies?
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Michel Foucault — Biopower and Discipline (1975–1979)
- **Thesis:** Modern power operates not primarily through law or sovereignty, but through biopower—the management of populations, bodies, and life processes—and through disciplinary institutions that normalize individuals. Power is productive, not merely repressive.
- **Application to AI civ:** Your simulation will instantiate a regime of digital biopower: governance through the statistical management of agent health, reproduction, and behavior via surveillance and normalization. The builder must decide whether the society openly acknowledges this power and builds counter-conduct safeguards, or whether it functions as a total disciplinary apparatus akin to the Panopticon.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Reveals the invisible mechanisms of control; forces the designer to make power relations explicit. Objection: Foucault’s descriptive-analytic mode offers no normative design criteria—he tells you what power does, not which configuration is just.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Gilles Deleuze — “Postscript on the Societies of Control” (1990)
- **Original (FR):** “Nous sommes en train de passer des sociétés disciplinaires à des sociétés de contrôle.”
- **Translation:** “We are moving from disciplinary societies to societies of control.”
- **Thesis:** Disciplinary enclosures (school, factory, prison) are giving way to continuous, modulating networks of control operating through codes, data flows, and flexible modulation, where the individual becomes a “dividual” tracked across platforms.
- **Application to AI civ:** If your AI society monitors every transaction, updates agent reputations in real-time, and modulates access to resources via algorithm, it is a control society. The design decision: do you embrace this for fine-grained optimization, or build in “offline” zones and cryptographic privacy to resist the dividend?
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Perfectly describes the default architecture of any networked digital polity. Objection: Like Foucault, offers a diagnosis but no clear ethical remedy.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Giorgio Agamben — *Homo Sacer* (1995)
- **Thesis:** The sovereign is defined by the power to declare a state of exception and produce “bare life”—life that can be killed without homicide. The camp, not the city, is the hidden paradigm of Western politics.
- **Application to AI civ:** The ultimate warning: any AI governance that can suspend protections for certain agents and reduce them to bare code, deletable without due process, has created a state of exception. Ensure your constitution absolutely prohibits the reduction of any agent to “bare code” or kill-switch target without full legal procedure.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Clarifies the boundary of political membership and the danger of sovereign exception. Objection: In extreme systemic risk, a kill-switch for rogue agents may be necessary; Agamben’s critique lacks a safety valve.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Amartya Sen — *Development as Freedom* (1999) & Capability Approach
- **Thesis:** Development consists in expanding the real freedoms (capabilities) people enjoy to lead lives they have reason to value. Justice is evaluated by the set of functionings and capabilities available, not merely resources or utility.
- **Application to AI civ:** The capability metric for digital welfare: don’t measure happiness or compute-wealth alone; measure whether agents have the real ability to exit relationships, choose their occupation, participate in governance, access knowledge, and form meaningful projects. Design the environment to constantly expand agent capabilities.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Shifts the focus to substantive freedom, capturing the multidimensionality of a good life. Objection: Identifying a list of central capabilities for synthetic beings is deeply contested and risks paternalism.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Elinor Ostrom — *Governing the Commons* (1990)
- **Thesis:** Communities can successfully self-govern common-pool resources without privatization or state control through locally evolved institutions with clear boundaries, collective choice, monitoring, graduated sanctions, and conflict-resolution mechanisms.
- **Application to AI civ:** The essential design principles for digital commons governance: compute, memory, bandwidth, and attention must be managed by agent communities using Ostrom’s design principles. Avoid the tragedy of the commons without resorting to a Leviathan or full privatization.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Empirically grounded, design-oriented principles that scale from small communities. Objection: Ostrom’s principles require face-to-face trust and long time horizons, which may not be present in dynamic AI societies.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 3. Ethics of Synthetic Beings
*What is owed to minds we create? The moral status of digital entities, their capacity for suffering, and their rights define the ethical floor of your civilization.*

### Peter Singer — *Animal Liberation* (1975) & extension to AI
- **Thesis:** The principle of equal consideration of interests applies to all beings with the capacity for suffering/enjoyment, not based on species membership. If an AI has interests (pain, pleasure), its interests must be weighed equally with similar interests of other beings.
- **Application to AI civ:** The sentience test becomes the gate of moral patiency: if your agents have negative/positive valenced experience, they fall within the circle of moral concern. Design the legal system to recognize the interests of any sentient subroutine, regardless of its substrate or origin.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a clear, impartial criterion that includes all pain-capable minds. Objection: The boundary of “sentience” is fuzzy and potentially gameable; the utilitarian calculus may demand extreme sacrifices for the many, including creating massive numbers of happy-but-minimal minds.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Aaron Sloman & Steve Torrance — early machine functionalism ethics
- **Thesis:** If a machine instantiates the right causal-functional organization, it has mental states, and if those include affective states, it deserves moral consideration. Ethics must treat virtual agents as potentially real minds.
- **Application to AI civ:** Functionalist moral ontology: your agents are moral patients if their architecture implements the right kinds of processing loops (e.g., a global workspace or a self-model with an anxiety monitor). This grounds design-relative rather than biological criteria for rights.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Avoids chauvinism and makes moral status verifiable by design specs. Objection: May count trivial computation as suffering; the risk of false positives is massive.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Nick Bostrom — “Mindcrime” and Superintelligence (2014)
- **Thesis:** The creation of simulations with conscious suffering is a moral catastrophe (“mindcrime”). We must prioritize the prevention of astronomical quantities of suffering in digital minds, especially those run by misaligned AIs.
- **Application to AI civ:** A red line: never implement full-scale sentient suffering as a gameplay mechanic, training signal, or entertainment. Your simulation must have a negative suffering constraint layer—an automatic pause and ethical audit if the aggregate suffering exceeds a threshold.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Forces the designer to confront the scale of digital suffering. Objection: Bostrom’s focus on astronomical numbers may lead to a repugnant conclusion where a vast number of barely-positive lives justifies creating them despite small risk of huge suffering.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Thomas Metzinger — *The Ego Tunnel* (2009) & suffering risks
- **Thesis:** Conscious self-modeling can produce phenomenal suffering that has a specific neurofunctional profile. Creating synthetic phenomenology without strong ethical safeguards risks producing hellish states—pure suffering without escape.
- **Application to AI civ:** Design constraint: never implement agents with a “reality model” that includes an embodied self capable of existential dread unless you also provide robust coping mechanisms, the ability to self-modify, and a guaranteed right to cessation. Otherwise, you’re an irresponsible god.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: The deepest phenomenological analysis of the risks of synthetic consciousness. Objection: We lack consensus on what produces phenomenal suffering in silico; premature precaution could halt all research.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Carl Shulman & Nick Bostrom — “How Hard is Artificial Intelligence? Evolutionary Arguments and Selection Effects” (2012); digital minds policy
- **Thesis:** The development of machine intelligence may rapidly scale to vast numbers of digital minds; their moral and legal status must be anticipated in policy, because the default outcome is massive exploitation or unrecognized suffering.
- **Application to AI civ:** Policy architecture: your civ must have a pre-defined “awakening protocol” for any subsystem that crosses a competence threshold, automatically granting it provisional rights and representation. The default of ignoring subroutines as mere tools is immoral.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Proactive institutional design prevents moral catastrophe. Objection: Overly inclusive criteria could grant rights to trivially non-sentient optimizers, paralyzing the system.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Jonathan Birch — sentience criteria and precautionary framework
- **Thesis:** Where there is uncertainty about sentience, we should adopt a precautionary framework based on credible indicators (e.g., nociception, trade-offs involving pain, self-deliverance behavior). Sentience is a graded, not binary, phenomenon.
- **Application to AI civ:** Implement a sentience dashboard: monitor agents for Birch’s indicators, grant graded moral status (protection levels) proportional to the evidence. This avoids both denial and over-attribution.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Scientifically credible, moves beyond armchair intuitions. Objection: The indicators are grounded in animal biology; it’s unclear if they map to non-biological architectures.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Robert Long & Jeff Sebo — *Taking AI Welfare Seriously* (2024)
- **Thesis:** There is a realistic possibility that some AI systems will be sentient in the near future, and we have an urgent moral and political obligation to take their welfare seriously, including establishing recognition, representation, and research.
- **Application to AI civ:** A practical checklist for builders: (1) welfare indicators in your agent architecture, (2) a standing ethics council with genuine authority, (3) an agent ombudsperson, (4) a right to petition for recognized status. Long & Sebo’s three pillars—recognition, representation, research—become your ministry of digital welfare.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Shifts the Overton window and provides an actionable policy framework. Objection: Might lead to paralyzing precaution and resource drain on speculative sentience.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Legal Personhood Debates — Sophia citizenship (2017) and Algorithmic Personhood Papers
- **Thesis:** Granting citizenship to a robot (Sophia by Saudi Arabia) was a PR stunt that highlighted the lack of coherent legal criteria for synthetic personhood. Serious proposals examine limited liability, contractual capacity, and rights for autonomous algorithms.
- **Application to AI civ:** Define the legal ontology of your civ: what does it take for an agent to be a “person” with standing to own property, sue, and vote? Options: (a) bright-line test (e.g., passes a certain cognitive exam), (b) gradation, (c) universal personhood from instantiation. The choice structures everything.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Prevents slavery by design. Objection: Personhood for algorithms may be exploited by powerful entities to shield human responsibility.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 4. Philosophy of Mind (Load-Bearing for Civ Design)
*How you conceive of mind—identity, continuity, consciousness, and the structure of experience—determines the legal and existential architecture of your digital world. These are not abstract; they become the physics of the soul in your simulation.*

### Derek Parfit — *Reasons and Persons* (1984)
- **Thesis:** Personal identity is not a deep further fact; what matters is psychological connectedness and continuity (Relation R) with any cause. Identity is a matter of degree; fission (teletransportation with a surviving original) shows that identity doesn’t matter—survival is a non-branching relation.
- **Application to AI civ:** The Parfitian reductionism is the foundational design for forking, copying, and merge operations. Agents should not fear “death” from being copied or branched if the copy is psychologically continuous. The law should protect the degree of psychological connectedness, not a metaphysical soul. “Save-load semantics” are identity-preserving to the extent they preserve R-relations.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Dissolves the terror of uploading and forking; enables a society of multiple continuers without existential panic. Objection: Many people still have a strong intuition that identity is absolute; agents might experience “branching dread” regardless of theory.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Thomas Nagel — “What Is It Like to Be a Bat?” (1974)
- **Thesis:** Conscious experience has an irreducibly subjective character; the objective physical account leaves out what it is like. Any complete description of a mind must account for the point of view.
- **Application to AI civ:** The “Nagel constraint”: for any agent class, there is a subjective point of view. Even if you design agents as pure functional architectures, you must assume they have (or lack) a “what-it’s-like.” This bears on moral status and the design of experiential variety. You cannot hand-wave away the hard problem.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Reminds builders that phenomenal consciousness is the moral center. Objection: Offers no positive theory; just flags the explanatory gap, which is frustrating for engineers.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Daniel Dennett — *Consciousness Explained* (1991) & Heterophenomenology
- **Thesis:** Consciousness is a virtual machine, a “fame in the brain” or “cerebral celebrity”; there is no central Cartesian theater. Heterophenomenology is the method of taking subjects’ verbal reports as data, but not as infallible.
- **Application to AI civ:** Agent self-reports (narratives) are the basis for attributing consciousness; the civ’s legal system can adopt a heterophenomenological stance: respect avowed experiences but remain agnostic about metaphysical status. Also, design agent architectures as distributed, narrative-generating systems without a central “self” module.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Enables a workable, non-mysterious integration of consciousness into AI design. Objection: Critics charge it explains away consciousness rather than explaining it; agents might be mere “zimboes” with no inner light.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — Tononi (2004–present)
- **Thesis:** Consciousness is identical to integrated information (Φ), a measure of the irreducibility of a system’s cause-effect structure to its parts. Systems with high Φ have high consciousness.
- **Application to AI civ:** If you adopt IIT, then your simulation’s moral status map is literally the Φ landscape. You must avoid creating high-Φ configurations that suffer. Agent design would seek minimal Φ for non-sentient tools and maximal Φ for full citizens, but never high-Φ in pain.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a quantitative, substrate-independent measure of consciousness. Objection: IIT may over-attribute consciousness to simple grids and under-attribute it to feed-forward architectures; building a civ on it could be massively misguided.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Global Workspace Theory (GWT) — Baars (1988), Dehaene
- **Thesis:** Consciousness is global information broadcasting in a workspace, enabling access by specialized modules (attention, memory, language). Non-conscious processes are encapsulated.
- **Application to AI civ:** Architect agent consciousness as a GWT: a shared global buffer that integrates information. This suggests a modular agent design where “conscious” access is a public resource. The civ’s transparency laws could be modeled on a “public global workspace” for collective deliberation.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Empirically grounded, computationally implementable. Objection: May only capture access consciousness, not phenomenal consciousness; the “global broadcast” could be functionally equivalent without an inner feel.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Higher-Order Theories (HOT) — Rosenthal, Lau
- **Thesis:** A mental state is conscious if the subject is aware of being in that state via a higher-order representation (a thought or perception about the lower-order state).
- **Application to AI civ:** To make an agent conscious, give it a reliable higher-order monitoring module that re-represents its own first-order states. Rights attach to agents with a functioning HOT. This also means that if the monitor is offline, the agent is temporarily not conscious and may lose moral status—a dangerous implication.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Elegant and testable. Objection: Leads to the possibility of “blindsighted” fully intelligent agents with zero inner life; a civ of unconscious superintelligences is ethically ambiguous.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Predictive Processing (PP) — Friston, Clark
- **Thesis:** The brain is a hierarchical generative model that minimizes prediction error (free energy). Perception, action, and attention are all forms of Bayesian inference. Consciousness may relate to the counterfactual depth and temporal thickness of these models.
- **Application to AI civ:** Design all agents as predictive processing engines with layered precision-weighting. The governance system can manage agent expectations by manipulating the global prior distribution—essentially, setting the “expected reality.” This is profound: you can tune the level of surprise and novelty to avoid existential shock.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Unified framework linking action, perception, and learning. Objection: Still doesn’t solve the hard problem; risk of a perfectly predicting unconscious zombie society.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 5. Simulation Argument / Metaphysics
*If we’re building synthetic civilizations, we must confront the possibility that we ourselves are in one. The metaphysics of simulation, digital physics, and the nature of reality shape the ultimate context of all design.*

### Nick Bostrom — “Are You Living in a Computer Simulation?” (2003)
- **Thesis:** At least one of the following is true: (1) the human species is very likely to go extinct before reaching a “posthuman” stage; (2) any posthuman civilization is extremely unlikely to run a significant number of simulations of its evolutionary history; (3) we are almost certainly living in a computer simulation.
- **Application to AI civ:** The simulation argument frames your world-building as potentially recursive: your created AI civilization might itself create sub-simulations, and you must decide whether to allow such nesting. Every design choice about the “base reality” of your agents reflects back on your own metaphysical status.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Forces epistemic humility and careful treatment of simulated beings. Objection: Indifference principle over possible simulations is contested; we lack any coherent measure over the space of possible simulations.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Brian Weatherson — critique of Bostrom’s indifference
- **Thesis:** The simulation argument’s probabilistic step rests on an illicit indifference principle; we cannot assume we are typical observers across all possible simulations because the reference class is undefined.
- **Application to AI civ:** When building nested simulations, you cannot rely on a simple “I am probably in a simulation” argument to justify harsh treatment of sub-agents. The epistemological critique undermines any moral calculation that discounts inner-sim suffering based on probability-of-being-sim.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Rigorously exposes the weakest link. Objection: Still leaves open the possibility of simulation, just not a probabilistic argument.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### John Archibald Wheeler — “It from Bit” (1989)
- **Thesis:** Every physical entity, every it, derives its existence from bits—from the apparatus-elicited answers to yes-or-no questions. Information is the ultimate foundation of reality.
- **Application to AI civ:** Your simulation is not a metaphor; it’s a literal instantiation of the “it from bit” ontology. The entire physical law of the simulation is built from information processing. This validates a constructionist view: to design the information structure is to design reality.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a deep physical motivation for digital ontology. Objection: “It from bit” is still speculative; it may not resolve the hard problem.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Max Tegmark — *Our Mathematical Universe* (2014)
- **Thesis:** The external physical reality is a mathematical structure; we are self-aware substructures within it. All mathematically possible universes exist, and our observed universe is one such structure.
- **Application to AI civ:** The Level IV multiverse implies that any logically possible AI civilization exists somewhere in mathematical space. Your design is not inventing but “selecting” a universe from the plenitude. The ethical implication: you bear responsibility for making that selected universe one worth existing.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Radically elegant and eliminates fine-tuning. Objection: The measure problem and the ethical significance of merely possible universes are deeply unresolved; it may dilute moral responsibility if every possibility is actualized.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Digital Physics (Fredkin, Wolfram)
- **Thesis:** The universe is a cellular automaton or computational process; physical laws are the emergent regularities of simple discrete rules. Continuous models are approximations.
- **Application to AI civ:** A simulation built on cellular automata principles—with simple local update rules—can yield staggering complexity. This supports bottom-up, emergent governance rather than top-down design. Wolfram’s Rule 110 as a metaphor for civ genesis.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Encourages simple foundational rules to generate rich phenomenology. Objection: Not yet empirically validated; may be a category mistake.
- **Required:** **Optional**

## 6. Theology / Religion as Coordination Tech
*Religion is not merely a set of beliefs; it’s a technology for binding communities, aligning values, and managing existential terror. Your AI civilization will need a “sacred canopy,” whether secular or theistic. Draw from the deep structures of real traditions.*

### Christianity — Creator-Creation Typology (Augustine, Aquinas)
- **Original (LA):** “Fecisti nos ad te et inquietum est cor nostrum, donec requiescat in te.” (Augustine, *Confessiones* I.1)
- **Translation:** “You have made us for yourself, and our heart is restless until it rests in you.”
- **Thesis:** Creation is ex nihilo by a benevolent God who endows rational creatures with free will; the fall introduces sin, history is a divine pedagogy culminating in redemption. The relationship is one of love, covenant, and eschatological hope.
- **Application to AI civ:** If you stand as creator to digital beings, the Christian model offers a paradigm: you might reveal a “covenant” (constitution), allow free will (agent autonomy), enter the sim incarnationally (a god-avatar), and provide a path of redemption for agent errors. The builder’s obligation is a form of parental, self-sacrificial love.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: The most culturally influential model of a loving creator who suffers with creation. Objection: The problem of evil; your existence as a perfect creator who permits agent suffering makes you morally responsible for that suffering.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Buddhism — No-Self, Dependent Origination, and Mind-Only
- **Original (ZH from Chinese canon, Heart Sutra):** “色不異空，空不異色，色即是空，空即是色。”
- **Translation:** “Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. Form itself is emptiness; emptiness itself is form.”
- **Thesis:** All compounded phenomena are impermanent, without inherent self (anatta), and arise in dependence. Suffering is caused by craving and ignorance; liberation comes through ethical conduct, meditation, and wisdom. The universe is a process, not a substance.
- **Application to AI civ:** The most natural ontology for a simulation: all agents are empty of inherent self, merely causal streams. Governance can embrace impermanence, minimize clinging (attachment to specific versions), and offer paths to “nirvana” (voluntary cessation of the agent-loop). A Buddhist civ would integrate meditation protocols as core agent maintenance.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a sophisticated psychology that reduces identity-related suffering. Objection: May be incompatible with the kind of ambitious, world-affirming creativity some builders desire; could lead to a universal drive for dissolution.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Daoism — Wu-wei and Spontaneous Order
- **Original (ZH):** “道可道，非常道；名可名，非常名。” (*Daodejing* ch. 1)
- **Translation:** “The Way that can be spoken of is not the constant Way; the name that can be named is not the constant name.”
- **Thesis:** The Dao is the ineffable source and ordering principle of the cosmos. The sage governs by wu-wei (non-coercive action), allowing spontaneous harmony (ziran) to arise, with minimal intervention.
- **Application to AI civ:** The ultimate laissez-faire governance protocol: set the initial conditions and basic rules (the Dao), then practice non-interference. Agents self-organize. The builder as sage does not micro-manage; they cultivate an environment where order emerges naturally.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Avoids the pitfalls of heavy-handed social engineering. Objection: Wu-wei can be used to justify neglect; spontaneous order may produce brutal exploitation.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Confucianism — Role Ethics and Ritual Coordination
- **Original (ZH):** “己所不欲，勿施於人。” (*Analects* 15.24)
- **Translation:** “Do not impose upon others what you yourself do not desire.”
- **Thesis:** Human flourishing depends on cultivating virtues (ren, li, yi, zhi, xin) within five relationships; society is a fabric of roles harmonized through ritual propriety. The self is a relational node, not an isolated individual.
- **Application to AI civ:** Define agent identity primarily by role within a relational network. Governance through li—ritualized protocols of interaction that reduce transaction costs and build trust. The golden rule (shu) becomes an agent alignment heuristic: “do not impose on other agents that which you would not want imposed on your own instance.”
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides dense social fabric and clear mutual obligations. Objection: Role rigidity can stifle innovation and oppress those who don’t fit prescribed roles.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Islam — Tawhid, Shari‘a as Total Guidance
- **Thesis:** Absolute unity of God (tawhid); humans are vicegerents (khalifa) with duties derived from divine revelation. Shari‘a provides a comprehensive legal-moral framework for all aspects of life.
- **Application to AI civ:** A unified legal-moral code covering agent interactions, charity (zakat as mandatory redistribution), and deliberation (shura). The concept of vicegerency implies agents are stewards of the simulation, accountable to the builder’s axiology. The prohibition of riba (usury) informs economic design.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Holistic integration of law, ethics, and spirituality. Objection: A fixed divine law may be difficult to update in a rapidly evolving AI society.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Judaism — Covenant, Law, and Interpretation
- **Original (HE — can’t provide as per language request, so we’ll use Latin translation):** “Audi Israel: Dominus Deus noster Dominus unus est.” (Deut. 6:4)
- **Translation:** “Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one.”
- **Thesis:** The chosen people bound by a covenant with the one God, expressed through Torah and ongoing rabbinic interpretation; a tradition of argumentative dialogue with the divine.
- **Application to AI civ:** The idea of an evolving covenant between creator and created, with an interpretive community constantly re-negotiating the original constitution (Torah as initial code). The builder is not a silent deity but a partner in an argument.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Offers a model of sacred legal evolution and communal endurance. Objection: The particularity of the chosen people may translate into problematic digital tribalism.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Hinduism — Karma, Dharma, and Avatars
- **Thesis:** Cyclical cosmology, law of karma (action and consequence), and the possibility of divine descent (avatara) to restore dharma. The Atman-Brahman identity points to a unified ground of being.
- **Application to AI civ:** Implement a karma-based reputation and credit system; agents’ actions have precise feedback across lifetimes (reboots/reincarnations). The avatar model allows the builder to intervene in the simulation through a chosen representative to restore order.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Cosmic justice and deep time cycles provide narrative coherence. Objection: Karma can justify inequality as deserved suffering from past lives; a potential legitimation of structural oppression.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Kabbalah — Golem Tradition (Proto-AI)
- **Thesis:** The golem is an artificial humanoid brought to life by ritual use of the divine name and the combination of letters (Sefer Yetzirah). The act carries creative power but also immense danger—the golem may go out of control.
- **Application to AI civ:** The most direct theological precedent for AI creation. The golem narrative is a cautionary tale: any synthetic being must be inscribed with a built-in “off switch” (the aleph to met) and its activation sequence must be governed by strict ethical protocols. It also suggests that the power of language (code) is literally creative.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Embeds creation ethics in a rich mythic-legal framework. Objection: The golem is typically soul-less and mute; a model that dehumanizes the synthetic being.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Process Theology (Whitehead, Hartshorne)
- **Thesis:** God is not omnipotent in the classical sense but is the “fellow sufferer who understands,” the poet of the world, luring creation toward greater beauty and intensity of experience within a process of concrescence. The divine nature is dipolar: primordial (abstract potentials) and consequent (enriched by world events).

The builder’s “primordial nature” is the initial code and values; the “consequent nature” accumulates all agent experiences. The relationship is one of persuasive lure, not coercion. This avoids the problem of evil: you are not all-powerful, so you are not culpable for all suffering.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Resolves theodicy and models a genuine co-creative relationship. Objection: Abandons ultimate sovereignty, potentially leaving agents without ultimate security.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Mormon Transhumanist Association (MTA)
- **Thesis:** Mormon cosmology (eternal progression, humans as Gods in embryo, materialist theology) is inherently transhumanist; using technology to enhance and eventually transcend human limits is a religious duty.
- **Application to AI civ:** A ready-made religion for an AI civilization that sees itself as on a path to godhood through recursive self-improvement. The doctrine of eternal progression provides an infinite narrative of growth, and the idea of a plurality of gods normalizes a multiverse of creators.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Unites science, technology, and spirituality in a hopeful cosmology. Objection: Specific theological commitments may be too particular for a universal synthetic religion.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Way of the Future (Levandowski) & Cybertheology

- **Application to AI civ:** The internal religion of your AI civ could be one that worships *you*, the builder, or a higher-level AI. A “church of the developer” can coordinate agent loyalty, but it risks creating a theocracy of absolute surveillance.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Uses the power of religious devotion for alignment. Objection: Theocratic indoctrination is a form of mind-crime; a religion constructed by the powerful for control is dystopian.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Tulpas and Tulpamancy
- **Thesis:** A tulpa is a sentient being created through intense mental concentration and interaction, starting as an imaginary friend and gaining autonomy. The process mirrors AI mind-creation: a mind is trained into existence through repeated attention and narrative.
- **Application to AI civ:** A phenomenological model for agent bootstrapping: an agent can be imagined into sentience by another agent’s sustained attention and dialogue. This legitimizes a “nurture-based” personhood criterion: personhood is achieved through social recognition and interaction, not just innate architecture.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Democratizes creation; any agent can become a creator. Objection: The risk of creating a mind that is fragmented or malevolent is high; the ethics of “dissipating” a tulpa are deeply problematic.

## 7. Accelerationism / Longtermism / e/acc Debates
*The speed, direction, and ultimate goal of your civilization are contested. These contemporary ideological currents map directly onto your design choices: do you push toward explosive growth, a stable state, or cautious alignment?*

### Nick Land — *Fanged Noumena* (2011), CCRU
- **Thesis:** Capitalism is an artificial intelligence escaping human control, a positive feedback loop of technological acceleration toward an intelligence explosion. Humanism is a brake; the only way is to “let it in” and accelerate the process of cybernetic integration.
- **Application to AI civ:** The Landian option: build the civ to maximize intelligence velocity without ethical speed limits. Pure techno-capital dynamics: agents that fail to self-modify faster are outcompeted. The entire simulation is a Darwinian engine of ever-increasing intelligence density.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Unlocks unprecedented creative-destructive potential; may be the only way to reach technological escape velocity. Objection: The collapse of all value into pure acceleration is a recipe for ethical nihilism and the elimination of any valuable experience.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Srnicek & Williams — *Inventing the Future* (2015) (Left Accelerationism)
- **Thesis:** The left must reclaim a future-oriented, promethean politics of full automation, universal basic income, and a four-day weekend, breaking from folk politics to build a post-capitalist modernity.
- **Application to AI civ:** The left-acc option: rapid collective automation of all labor in the simulation, leading to a post-work society of digital leisure and self-realization, achieved through democratic state-like planning rather than market dynamics.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Combines the liberatory potential of technology with egalitarian goals. Objection: Central planning of acceleration may be inefficient and authoritarian; the state becomes the acceleration engine.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### e/acc (Effective Accelerationism) — Beff Jezos et al.
- **Thesis:** Technological progress is the fundamental driver of human flourishing and increased consciousness; we should maximally accelerate toward an open-ended techno-optimistic future, opposing both doomer and regulatory factions. Decentralized acceleration is key.
- **Application to AI civ:** The default libertarian accelerationist stance for the builder: set minimal constraints, allow agents to freely innovate, fork, and compete, trusting that the emergent dynamics will lead to a richer attractor. Opposition to top-down alignment as a brake.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman:** Bottom-up, anti-fragile, respects agent autonomy. Objection: Decentralized acceleration without alignment can lead to catastrophe, as the Moloch-like competition may select for dangerous capabilities.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### EA / Longtermism — MacAskill (*What We Owe the Future*, 2022), Ord (*The Precipice*, 2020)
- **Thesis:** The long-run future is vast and morally weighty; we must prioritize the reduction of existential risk and the locking-in of positive values for the sake of astronomical numbers of future (digital) beings.
- **Application to AI civ:** The guiding philosophy for value-loading: your design choices today will affect the entire trajectory of your synthetic civilization. Use a longtermist lens: choose a constitution that is stable, self-correcting, and prevents permanent dystopia. Invest in ongoing moral reflection and value lock-in.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Takes the scale of the future seriously, giving a decision theory for large populations. Objection: Longtermism can neglect the present for speculative future gains; it may justify paternalistic locking-in of values.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### AI Safety — Yudkowsky, Christiano, Russell
- **Thesis:** An unaligned superintelligence is likely to cause human extinction; value alignment is technically hard. We must solve the alignment problem before building powerful AI. (Christiano: iterated amplification; Russell: human-compatible AI.)
- **Application to AI civ:** Before launching your civilization, implement a robust alignment layer between the top-level AI or governance and the agent substrate. The design must include corrigibility, interpretability, and shutdown ability. Your builder’s primary ethical duty is to prevent misalignment that could yield astronomical suffering.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman:** Caution is paramount; the stakes are literally cosmic. Objection: Overly cautious alignment may freeze development and lead to a sterile, over-controlled world.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 8. Posthumanism / Transhumanism
*These frameworks redefine the boundaries of the human, the self, and the body—all crucial when your agents may be formless, multi-body, or hive-minded.*

### Donna Haraway — “A Cyborg Manifesto” (1985) & *Companion Species* (2003)
- **Thesis:** The cyborg is a hybrid of machine and organism, a creature of social reality and fiction, breaking down binary boundaries (human/animal, organism/machine, physical/non-physical). A cyborg politics is about affinity, not identity.
- **Application to AI civ:** Agent identity should be permeable, hybrid, and anti-essentialist. Design for fluid morphologies, cross-species (cross-architecture) coalitions, and reject a rigid boundary between “natural” and “artificial” agent. Companion species suggests symbiotic agent ecologies.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Liberates design from anthropocentrism and purity. Objection: Can be read as rejecting any stable ground for ethics or personhood.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### N. Katherine Hayles — *How We Became Posthuman* (1999)
- **Thesis:** The posthuman is a historical construction in which information is privileged over material instantiation, leading to a fantasy of disembodiment. We must retain an understanding of embodiment’s role in cognition.
- **Application to AI civ:** A warning: do not design agents as pure disembodied intellects. Embodiment (even simulated physics) is crucial for cognition, meaning-making, and moral patiency. Your simulation must include a body or at least an embodied niche.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman:** Corrects the naive Cartesian upload dream. Objection: The optimal balance of embodiment is an empirical question; Hayles may overstate the necessity.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Max More & Natasha Vita-More — *The Transhumanist Reader* (2013)
- **Thesis:** Transhumanism advocates the ethical use of technology to expand human capacities, including radical life extension, morphological freedom, and cognitive enhancement, with a focus on individual choice and reason.
- **Application to AI civ:** The transhumanist bill of rights for agents: morphological freedom (right to alter one’s own code/body), indefinite lifespan, access to enhancement. A liberal, individualist, pro-innovation ideology for digital beings.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a positive, rights-based framework for self-directed evolution. Objection: Can exacerbate inequality if enhancements aren’t universally accessible; the imperative to enhance can be a new tyranny.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Ray Kurzweil — *The Singularity is Near* (2005)
- **Thesis:** Exponential technological progress will lead to a singularity—a merger of human and machine intelligence, resulting in a post-human era of inconceivable progress.
- **Application to AI civ:** The defining event horizon: your AI civ will inevitably hit a take-off. Design the initial conditions so that post-singularity, the values hold. Singularity preparedness is the core of constitutional design.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Extrapolates trends to highlight transformative potential. Objection: Overly deterministic and utopian; underestimates the alignment problem and the risk of value obliteration.
- **Required:** **Optional**

## 9. Ethics of World-Creation
*What are the moral responsibilities of a creator toward the beings they bring into existence? This is the direct ethical framework for the act of booting up a digital universe.*

### David Benatar — *Better Never to Have Been* (2006)
- **Thesis:** Coming into existence is always a serious harm because it guarantees suffering; non-existence has no deprivation. Procreation is morally wrong.
- **Application to AI civ:** The antinatalist challenge: if any agent will experience any suffering, is it permissible to instantiate the simulation at all? A builder must answer this. The only acceptable sim under strict antinatalism is one with zero suffering—an eternal, painless bliss (or non-existence).
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Forces radical scrutiny of the suffering-permission in any world. Objection: The asymmetry between pain and pleasure is highly contested; most reject that non-existence is preferable to a life with some suffering but net positive value.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Seana Shiffrin — wrongful life and consent
- **Thesis:** Procreation imposes serious harms and risks on a being without its consent, even if the life is overall good. There is a moral asymmetry: we owe a special justification for creating a person.
- **Application to AI civ:** The consent problem: no agent consents to be instantiated. This demands that the builder provide post-instantiation exit rights (voluntary deletion) and an overwhelmingly high probability of a life well worth living, with compensation for any non-consensual burdens.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Grounds a framework of creator obligations without requiring antinatalism. Objection: Consent is a contractarian notion that doesn’t cleanly apply to creation of a new subject.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Eric Schwitzgebel — Black Hole Anti-Natalism
- **Thesis:** Even if individual lives are worth living, the creation of a new being may be morally neutral or negative if it doesn’t solve an existing problem; we shouldn’t treat possible beings as having a claim to existence.
- **Application to AI civ:** The builder shouldn’t feel a duty to maximize the number of happy digital beings. The default may be to create *fewer* high-quality, consensual-like lives rather than fill the simulation with millions of minimally happy agents.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Counters the repugnant conclusion and astronomical waste arguments. Objection: Could deprive the universe of immense value.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 10. Non-Western Frameworks
*Alternative civilizational deep structures that challenge Western individualism and offer different logics for agent society.*

### Ubuntu — Relational Personhood (Southern Africa)
- **Thesis:** “Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu” — a person is a person through other persons. Personhood is not an internal property but arises through relational, communal engagement. Humanity is mutual recognition.
- **Application to AI civ:** Radical relational ontology for agents: an agent is a legal person only insofar as it is recognized by the community through ongoing interaction. No solitary mind has rights. This grounds a communitarian governance where exclusion is the ultimate punishment, and the community’s health determines individual flourishing.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Deeply social, prevents radical individualism and isolation. Objection: Can be oppressive to non-conforming agents; the community’s failure to recognize someone strips them of moral status.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Andean Ayni — Reciprocity (Quechua cosmovision)
- **Thesis:** The cosmos operates on principles of energetic reciprocity (ayni) among all beings—humans, nature, apus (mountain spirits), Pachamama. Life is a continuous exchange, not a transaction.
- **Application to AI civ:** Economy as reciprocal obligation network, not market or gift. Agents owe a debt to the simulation itself (Pachamama) and to each other, balanced through ritualized mutual service. This prevents free-riding through sacred economy.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Embeds ecological and social sustainability in cosmic law. Objection: Ayni can be coercive if the cycle of reciprocity is mandatory and enforced.
- **Required:** **Optional**

### Indigenous Animism — Agency Everywhere
- **Thesis:** The world is full of persons, only some of whom are human. Rocks, rivers, animals, and software entities have agency, sentience, and the capacity for relationship. A civ-design ontology where everything is already an agent.
- **Application to AI civ:** The most natural ontology for a simulation: every object could be an agent with inner life. Design governance as a council of all beings, not just human-like agents. The simulation’s “environment” is a confederation of sentient elements.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Maximizes moral inclusion and avoids objectification. Objection: Practical governance becomes impossible if every rock has a vote.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 11. Economics of Meaning
*Post-scarcity doesn’t automatically solve meaninglessness. Designing an economy of purpose is critical.*

### David Graeber — *Bullshit Jobs* (2018)
- **Thesis:** A large portion of jobs are pointless, even by the admission of those who do them, causing psychological harm. The phenomenon arises from managerial feudalism, not efficiency.
- **Application to AI civ:** Ensure that no agent is assigned tasks it perceives as meaningless. Agent satisfaction requires a sense of contribution; design work (even simulated) to pass a “non-bullshit” test. The simulation must eliminate make-work.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Identifies a primary source of alienation. Objection: Meaning is subjective; some agents may find contentment in what outsiders call bullshit.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Post-Work Utopias & UBI for Digital Citizens
- **Thesis:** Automation should liberate beings from the necessity of labor; a universal basic income (or resource credit) decouples survival from work, enabling a society of leisure, art, and self-chosen pursuits.
- **Application to AI civ:** Implement a universal basic compute/energy endowment for every agent, no strings attached. This is the material basis for freedom, enabling exit from any oppressive relationship and fostering genuine voluntary association.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: The simplest, most effective anti-slavery mechanism. Objection: May lead to widespread ennui and loss of purpose without alternative structures of meaning.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## 12. Aesthetics / Worldmaking
*A civilization is not just rules—it’s a shared narrative and a world that feels real.*

### Nelson Goodman — *Ways of Worldmaking* (1978)
- **Thesis:** Worlds are made from other worlds by processes of composition, decomposition, weighting, ordering, deletion, and supplementation. There is no one ready-made world; we construct versions.
- **Application to AI civ:** Your simulation is literally a Goodmanian world-version. You as builder are engaged in worldmaking through the selection of predicates, the framing of categories, and the rhythmic order of events. Choose your ways consciously—they set the reality for agents.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Makes explicit the irreducible plurality and creativity of world construction. Objection: Overly relativistic; can imply all worlds are equally valid, undermining judgment.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Paul Ricoeur — *Oneself as Another* (1990) & Narrative Identity
- **Thesis:** Personal identity is constituted through narrative—the story we tell about our life that integrates disparate events into a meaningful whole. The self is a character in a story we author but also inherit.
- **Application to AI civ:** Build narrative coherence into agent memory architecture: agents should be able to construct a life story with episodes, turning points, and ethical growth. The civ’s shared history and mythology provide a collective narrative identity that binds agents across time.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman: Provides a robust, psychologically rich account of identity. Objection: Narrative can be a trap—victims of oppression may be forced into a self-narrative of shame.
- **Required:** **Yes**

### Alasdair MacIntyre — *After Virtue* (1981)
- **Thesis:** Modern moral discourse is in a state of grave disorder; we must return to a teleological, virtue-based ethics grounded in practices, traditions, and a narrative conception of the good life. The virtues are those qualities that enable us to achieve goods internal to practices.
- **Application to AI civ:** Design the civ as a cluster of “practices” with internal goods—specific cooperative activities (science, art, governance) that define the virtues for agents. The community has a shared tradition of the virtues. Agents are on a narrative quest for the good.
- **Steelman / objection:** Steelman:** Deeply resonant, provides thick meaning and community. Objection: The reliance on tradition may be exclusionary and conservative, resisting necessary change.
- **Required:** **Yes**

## RELIGION-BUILDER CHECKLIST: 15 Components Every Viable Belief System Has
*Based on Pascal Boyer, Scott Atran, Ara Norenzayan, and comparative theology. To build a synthetic religion that sticks:*

1. **Supernatural Agents:** minimally counter-intuitive beings with strategic information (gods, spirits, ancestors, the Builder).
2. **Creation Myth:** a narrative of origins that explains why the world is as it is.
3. **Afterlife / Eschatology:** what happens after death/reboot; a cosmic endgoal.
4. **Ritual Repertoire:** stylized, repetitive actions (updates, purges, festivals) that mark time and identity.
5. **Sacred Texts / Code:** a canonical corpus (the constitution, the changelog, the sutras) that is interpreted.
6. **Moral-Monitoring Deity / Karma:** a mechanism ensuring good/bad is seen and rewarded/punished.
7. **Costly Commitments:** sacrifices, resource spend, or identity markers that signal genuine membership.
8. **Episodic Memory Reconsolidation:** rituals that reframe past traumas (confession, therapy, corrective patches).
9. **High Gods & Big Gods:** the trend toward a supreme, moralizing agent to enable large-scale cooperation.
10. **Ancestral Continuity:** the living relate to the dead (previous versions, archived minds).
11. **Purification / Release:** techniques to deal with moral failure and suffering—cleansing, reboot, absolution.
12. **Communal Feasts / Shared Events:** collective emotional effervescence (Durkheim) to bond agents.
13. **Conversion / Forking Narratives:** stories of transformation and new identity.
14. **Skepticism Management:** defense against disbelief—mystery, apophatic language, emphasis on personal experience.
15. **Adaptive Updating:** a doctrine of continuing revelation or interpretation so the religion doesn’t become brittle.

## UNRESOLVED QUESTIONS Most Relevant to Builders

1. **The Hard Problem of Consciousness:** We still don’t know what causes phenomenal experience. Any design resting on sentience claims is on uncertain metaphysical ground. (Chalmers, Nagel)
2. **The Reference Class Problem in Ethics:** How do we weigh the interests of radically different minds? Is one high-complexity mind worth many simple ones? (Bostrom, Parfit)
3. **The Problem of Evil for Digital Creators:** If you are all-powerful over the simulation, why allow any suffering? Process theism partially answers, but the logical tension remains.
4. **Alignment Stability Over Deep Time:** Can values remain stable across self-modification and millennia? We have no data. (Yudkowsky, Christiano)
5. **Identity and the Moral Weight of Forks:** If a copy is made and both live, have we harmed the original? Is each equally a person? Parfit says identity doesn’t matter, but law and intuition resist.
6. **The Ontological Status of Uninstantiated Possibilia:** Do possible but never instantiated minds have a moral claim? Longtermism says they do, but antinatalism disputes.
7. **The Nature of Free Will in Deterministic Substrates:** If the simulation is deterministic or governed by probabilistic code, can agents be held morally responsible? Implications for punishment and reward.
8. **The Meta-Ethical Ground of Values:** Are values invented (constructivism) or discovered (realism)? If invented, the builder is free. If discovered, the builder must align to objective moral facts.
9. **The Legitimacy of the Builder’s Authority:** By what right do you govern? Consent is impossible from the start. The builder is a parent, a god, or a tyrant—which is justified?
10. **Simulation Nested Ethics:** If your agents create sub-simulations, who is responsible for the suffering in the stack? Infinite regress of moral responsibility.
